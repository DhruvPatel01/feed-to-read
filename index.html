If you have Vim, you also have xxd. Its primary use cases are (1) the basis for a hex editor due to its -r reverse option that can unhexdump its previous output, and (2) a data embedding tool for C and C++ (-i). The former provides Vim’s rudimentary hex editor functionality. The second case is of special interest to w64devkit: xxd -i appears in many builds that embed arbitrary data.
